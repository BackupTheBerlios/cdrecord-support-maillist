According to:

http://syslinux.zytor.com/wiki/index.php/ISOLINUX

  "only uses the plain ISO 9660 filenames, i.e. it does not support
   Rock Ridge or Joliet filenames."

and:

    "*  Use the following command to create your ISO image: 

     mkisofs -o output.iso \
             -b isolinux/isolinux.bin -c isolinux/boot.cat \
             -no-emul-boot -boot-load-size 4 -boot-info-table \
             CD_root
    "


Given that isolinux claims to not even be able to see
the Rock Ridge extensions, could the mere presence of
the RR support files be causing the problem irrespective
of their internal permissions/user ID/etc. settings?

If so, this suggests that even the "plainer" -R option
(instead of -r which does other things too) will now
cause isolinux to barf. Does it?

There may be some helpful info here:

http://www.911cd.net/forums/lofiversion/index.php/t23099.html

 "Remember XP boot sector may fail at rock ridge."
.
.
 "Thanks to Icecube, chain.c32 can chainload setuplr.bin at CD now:
  ... Extension Rock ridge and chain.c32 is possible now."


In any case, this probably is an isolinux change/issue and not
a problem with mkisofs.
