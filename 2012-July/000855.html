Omega Weapon <omegaphil at gmail.com> wrote:

> Right, I have done more work on this - since I am calling the correct
> mkisofs and cdrecord direct, I now get 2x burning - I assume this means
> the strange defect recovery stuff is no longer happening? I had
> previously told k3b to use cdrecord whereever possible, perhaps
> growisofs was somehow to blame for the previous slow burning.

growisofa operates on formatted media and this enforces a defect handling that 
makes writes slow.


> So, now I am in a position where the drive probably doesnt recognise the
> media, and subsequently burns at 2x rather than the media's rated 6x. To
> counter this I used the following:
>
> /opt/schily/bin/cdrecord -v -multi -fs=8m -driveropts=forcespeed
> -speed=4 -dev=/dev/sr0 "/mnt/Storage_1/Temporary Storage
> Folder/Processing Folder/Burning/disc_image.iso"
>
> However, cdrecord says 'Starting to write CD/DVD/BD at speed 0 in real
> SAO mode for multi session' - presumably if the force speed stuff works,
> it should say 4x? It then proceeds to burn at 2x.
>
> Am I correct in understanding that this is wrong? The drive reports that
> it supports forcespeed.

Cdrecord depends on the speed selection of the drive. You may request a write 
speed from the drive but the drive may ignore this request and select a lower 
speed.

The actual speed depends on the constraints and the drive/media pairing is one 
of the criteria.
