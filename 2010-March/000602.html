cdrecord -minfo just gives you information about the current state of the
medium. There is no relation to the compatibility between media and drive.

Let me just comment the last line: 12x DVD-RAM Media is only sold in Japan.
Due to the non-grantable media quality for 12x DVD-RAM media, the related 
DVD-RAM media is not sold in e.g. Europe.

> So when I bought this drive at least it claimed compatibility with the
> 9 types of media above. Note that I'm not talking about the speed it
> says it writes at, just compatibility - that presumably if I put any
> one of those 9 media types in the drive I have a chance of writing it
> at some speed.
>
>    My question is who do I get that list for an unknown drive in a
> random computer. I walk around the house here an I have 8 PCs. They
> all have different drives in them but I don't know what media types
> each can read and write.

You may try to get the list of related media for your drive from the 
manufacturer of the drive. 

If the claim about e.g. DVD-RAM was made _in_ Europe, you gould give your drive 
back to the seller as it is impossible for you to buy the related media.

>    In the case of the DVD-R that wasn't readable (and it was DVD-R)
> I'm pretty sure the drive just doesn't support DVD-R media but I don't
> know how to determine that. For instance, I could do DVD+R DL backups
> here but then wouldn't be able to read it there because that drive
> cannot do DL.

DVD+R/DL media was a real problem when it was new. There have been many cheep 
blanks that caused a write abort after the write process passed the layer break.
I have no current information about the current state for dual layer writing.
