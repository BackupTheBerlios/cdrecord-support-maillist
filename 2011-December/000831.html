> Because I have no idea it's unsupported! Why is the cryptic SCSI address used instead of one of the intuitive names of the device? If the software needs the SCSI address, why can't it derive it from the name of the device?

You of course know this as cdrecord warned you because of the dev= parameter 
you used. You even quoted the warning (see below.....)

Linux has a cryptic driver interface that includes more than one driver per 
CD-ROM drive. If you use the OS-independent SCSI address notation (or if you 
only have one drive and ommit the dev= option completely - see man page), 
cdrecord (libscg) is able to select the best driver for your drive. If you force
cdrecord (via dev=/dev/*) to use a suboptimal kernel driver, you get suboptimal 
results.

> > Why do you use an unsupported dev= parameter?
> > Setting dev= to a named driver instead of using the standard SCSI addresses may 
> > force Linux to use a bad driver (note that Linux implements more than one 
> > driver to access CD-ROM type drives).
> >
> > Why do you use tsize= at all and why do you use such a strange value?
> Because one of the notes said I should.
