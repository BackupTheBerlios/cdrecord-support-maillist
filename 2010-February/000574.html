Lucas Levrel <llevrel at yahoo.fr> wrote:

> The way I understand it is: cdrecord writes the given data onto disc, 
> whatever they are. With *.wav you get a TOC and tracks of audio data: 

This is not true.

wave files are headerful audio files. If the target medium is a CD, then
cdrecord strips off the header and writes the pure audio data in the proper
byte order to the CD advertizing the related track as audio track.

> that's enough for a player to play it. With File.iso you get a TOC and a 
> track containing a filesystem+data: that's enough for an OS to recover the 
> files. With Data.file you get a TOC and a track of raw data (the contents 
> of Data.file): an OS has no clue on what these data are (I think even the 
> filename isn't on the disc)! But if you know what the data are, I guess 
> you can recover the file with any tool able to read raw stuff, like dd.

A DVD does not know special type of data streams. Every data on a DVD is just 
pure data and it is up to the application to properly interpret it.
