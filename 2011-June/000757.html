This is probably due to a limited read speed "riplock" anti-piracy
protection in the firmware.

You can use the MediaCodeSpeedEdit application:

http://ala42.cdfreaks.com/MCSE/

to patch a firmware file and then flash the patched result into the
drive to remove this limit.


For myself, I like the NEC/Optiarc line of drives which have readily
available "Liggy and Dee" modified firmware:

http://liggydee.cdfreaks.com/page/en/

as well as a firmware flasher for Linux (although not open source).
