  Bob,

I don't understand. What exactly do you mean by "do not allow this"?

According to:

http://www.myce.com/review/optiarc-ad-7240s-dvd-burner-review-15981/Authors-page-9/

the Optiarc AD-7240S does not come with CSS riplock, and thus should read
at full speed right out of the box, and will rip a DVD in about 5 minutes.

Now, the newer 7260S is basically (or maybe even entirely!) the same
internal hardware as the 7240S, they even have crossflashed firmware
between the two:

http://club.myce.com/f107/recommend-me-good-dvd-dl-burner-314020/#post2532131

If anything, the newer Optiarc drives tend not to have riplock anymore,
so I don't think the 7260S has riplock, and indeed that is what they are
claiming on Ebay:

http://cgi.ebay.com/24X-PC-INTERNAL-SATA-CD-DVD-RW-DUAL-LAYER-BURNER-DRIVE-/170618791566

I agree with you that riplock is a dumb idea, does nothing to deter
piracy, yet is an annoyance to many users. I can see where they would
want to reduce drive noise, but doing it that way partially defeats
the purpose of buying a faster drive (we still get faster write speeds
though).
