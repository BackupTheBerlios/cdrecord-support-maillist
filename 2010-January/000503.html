I generally like them too, but if they are on the same server, they
(well, at least the MD5s) can be easily altered too. MD5's are also
handy for spotting corrupted files. It's not so common these days,
but I have seen modem links introduce data errors. (Although this can
be usually spotted during an attempted package decompression.) For the
case at hand, it also makes it easy to spot versions that have been
altered (but I don't know if cdrtools is even carried/mirrored 
anywhere else).

At any rate, tis a simple matter (at least for one file) for Joerg
to download cdrtools (at least the latest release) and directly
compare that to his original upload.
