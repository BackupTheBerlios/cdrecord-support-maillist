Sure, MD5s can be altered if they are only on the same server.
For signatures, you'd need the signing key, though, which (I hope) would
be guarded, and have a decent passphrase.

> At any rate, tis a simple matter (at least for one file) for Joerg
> to download cdrtools (at least the latest release) and directly
> compare that to his original upload.

Yup. And assuming that such backups exist, a comparison can be made with
backups done before the breakin.
But you need forensics for that, figuring out how and when they got in,
what backdoors they installed, and plugging these holes. For that, you
need to take the servers offline and at least dump the disks for later
examination. And generally, the servers shouldn't go back online until the
holes are plugged.
It really shouldn't be too demanding for Joerg to state if such things are
done. These are standard, professional procedures in case of a server
compromise, after all.
Red herrings like pointing to alleged misreporting at heise.de or pointing
to alleged wrong signing at gnu.org don't instill confidence that the
aftermath of *this* incident is handled in a professional manner.
"Officer, the guy in front of me was going faster" isn't effective at
getting out of a speeding ticket...
