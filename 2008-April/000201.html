Well, some hardware does not correctly implement the SATA layer (I believe it 
is called ehci) and for this reason, Solaris does not yet support ATAPI over
SATA for those chips. You need to tell the BIOS to emulate PATA for the SATA
interfaces. The fact that Solaris does not yet support ATAPI over your SATA
interface could be a hint for a Linux kernel bug....

As I told above, Solaris does not emulate SCSI, you need hard disks that
speak SCSI and you need a SATA interface that has a driver with native ATAPI 
support. If Solaris does not support ATAPI in native mode, this is a hint that 
the Linux driver may have problems with your HW. I have a PATA computer at home 
that gives similar problems (Illegal field in CDB although the SCSI command is 
100% correct) with Linux but everything works fine if the same hardware and same
cdrecord version is running on Solaris or SCO unix.


> Therefore, I do not see how the Linux kernel is my problem, or
> OpenSolaris is the answer. Solaris can't even detect the drive.

The Linux kernel most likely bastardizes the send cue sheet command before 
forwarding it to the drive. This is why I like to see the same test on Solaris.
Please switch your BIOS into PATA emulation mode and again boot Solaris in ram 
only mode.
