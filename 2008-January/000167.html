Very true.  AVCHD is a video format more than a disk or filesystem  
specification.  However, it is built on top of Blu-ray.

http://www.blu-raydisc.com/assets/downloadablefile/3_filesystem-12836.pdf

Authoring of Blu-ray (and hence AVCHD DVDs) requires UDF 2.5.

> From what I heard about UDF-2.5 vs. UDF-2.0, the main difference is  
> that
> UDF-2.5 allows to support life recording in video recorders and later
> to edit the content.

Well, I guess the full differences would be found in this document:
http://www.osta.org/specs/pdf/201250diffs.pdf

There is the Metadata partition, which is not in 2.01.  I think that  
is the biggest change between the versions.

> A stand alone video player is not expected to depend on these  
> features, so why
> would we need support for something that cannot be used with mkisofs?

Stand-alone Blu-ray players apparently do depend on the UDF 2.5.  Over  
at the doom9 forums, there are a number of threads about it too  
(although I do not necessarily condone their motives).

Current PC solutions require Nero or similar software to burn UDF 2.5  
disks.
