The clone feature (which is in readcd and cdrecord) has been added 12 years ago 
to permit to create copies of CDs with unknown and complex structure, such as 
game CDs. To be more specific: at that time, I tried to copy a video CD using 
"cdrdao" that claimed to be able to maked copies from video CDs. As it turned 
out that this was just a marketing gag, I needed to implement something in 
cdrtools that was able to make copies from Video CDs and this is what the clone
feature was made for.

The official cdrecord was indeed limited by not having source for DVD support 
for some time, but this was a result of a NDA with Pioneer that prevented me 
from making things OSS as long as those things were not widely known - e.g. 
until even companies like Nero did learn how to write DVDs without my help ;-)

So in theory, I could have made the DVD stuff OSS a year earlier than I 
actually did. The reason for this delay was that some people tried to blackmail 
me and I was not willing to let things look as if they have been successful.

Anyway, the missing (in the OSS) feature was freely available for everyone via 
precompiled binaries.
