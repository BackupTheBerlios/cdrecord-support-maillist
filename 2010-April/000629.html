I have posted about this issue on the Linux Kernel Mailing List:

http://lkml.org/lkml/2010/4/20/256

but have not received any responses yet.

Other links with info I ran across include:

http://blogs.gnome.org/cneumair/2006/02/11/ioctl-fsync-how-to-flush-block-device-buffers/

http://lkml.indiana.edu/hypermail/linux/kernel/0211.2/1485.html

http://aplawrence.com/Linux/buffer_cache.html


On my older system 

blockdev --flushbufs /dev/dvd

results in

BLKFLSBUF: Function not implemented


I have not tried: 

sync
echo 3 > /proc/sys/vm/drop_caches

Note that this affects the entire block device system (flushing
the buffers of every drive) and requires a 2.6.16 or later kernel.


If anyone learns anything else, please do post it here and let us
all know.
