This may just be the fact that I'm lazy, and now officially getting
not just older but actually old, but I think sometimes there's a real
problem with folks writing documents wherein some feel that more
written somehow imparts more knowledge. It's not the case, at least in
my opinion. I can accept that many of our Linux docs come from folks
all around the world. Some are not native English speakers and I don't
expect everything to be written like we speak in California. Heck,
many people think we don't speak English anyway.

What is really needed but seldom seems to exist is a very small
document that lists commands by specific task. In the case of the
cdrecord family of programs it might look a bit like:

Copy a data CD to iso
Burn a dataCD from iso
Rip an audio CD to files
Burn an audio CD from files
Create an iso of a Windows program that's bootable from VMWare
Rip a movie from DVD to disk

etc., etc., etc.

I truly appreciate the work that everyone does in Open Source, but I'm
not sure many developers have a clue what it's like ot be a user. I
don't think it's realistic to think that anyone should have to study
and memorize a 2000 line man page doc or dig around on their PCs for
text file documents to find the answer to a simple question. That said
I respect anyone telling me that's what I should do, and it's my right
to listen or not.

Cheers,
Mark
