In my later college days, I lived in an apartment that had a couple
machines linked via ethernet. One day, there was a big lightning
storm. A large bolt hit somewhere within a mile and I smelled smoke
inside my place.

Upon investigation, modems in both machines were fried from the
surge on the telephone line. (Since then, I always disconnect phone
and cable lines when a lightning storm approaches.)

However, the really strange thing is that I lost two ports on
the ethernet hub, although the ethernet cards in both machines
were fine. 

Upon further investigation, I found a place where a spark had
jumped *through the phone cord* and into the ethernet cable it was
lying against on the floor, destroying the ethernet hub chip that
handled that cable. Sheeeezzz.
